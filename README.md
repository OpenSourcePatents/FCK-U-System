What is FCK-U?

The FCK-U System is a comprehensive vehicle safety and comfort architecture designed specifically for next-generation EV platforms — such as the Tesla Cybertruck — that utilize steer-by-wire and drive-by-wire.
 * Total Isolation: The passenger cabin is mechanically decoupled from the vehicle frame via a gimbaled swivel pivot located at the cabin’s dynamic Center of Gravity.
 * Tri-Stage Suspension: A cooperative isolation system using three independently controllable stages — ECU-controlled electromagnetic dampers (E-CESA), dual-angled linear generator struts, and an active pivot bearing — coordinated by a Conflict Resolver running at 1,000 Hz to prevent inter-stage resonance.
 * Active Kinetic Safety: In an unavoidable crash, the system uses a four-point omnidirectional pyrotechnic breakaway to punch the cabin away from the impact vector. The direction of displacement is calculated from impact azimuth data in real time.
 * Fire Barrier and Flotation: The cabin floor is a structural composite skid plate with a ceramic outer shell rated for temperatures over 1,100°C, protecting occupants from traction battery fires. The sealed foam core provides positive buoyancy — allowing the cabin to float in water in the event of a water-immersion crash.
 * Battery Separation: The cabin physically separates from the traction battery pack on impact. If the battery burns, it burns alone — away from the occupants.
 * Self-Sustaining Power: Every suspension movement is captured via a dual-loop regenerative system that charges the main traction battery and a dedicated 2 kWh LFP Cabin Survival Battery reserved exclusively for post-crash safety functions.
 * Federated Dual-ECU: A cabin-resident secondary ECU operates in warm standby during normal driving and assumes full authority over steer-by-wire, brake-by-wire, doors, windows, and emergency communications upon cabin separation.
 * Wireless Redundancy: Post-separation vehicle control is maintained via a low-latency 60GHz wireless link with a three-node antenna diversity array on each side of the separation plane. End-to-end latency: 21–29 ms.
